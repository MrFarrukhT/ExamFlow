RECORDING IN PROGRESS

Part 1: Interview (2-3 minutes)

The examiner asks each candidate questions in turn. Answer naturally, giving reasons and examples where you can.

1. What's your name? Where are you from?
2. Tell me about your family.
3. What do you enjoy doing at the weekend?
4. Do you prefer spending time indoors or outdoors? Why?
5. What kind of music do you like listening to?
6. Tell me about a place you like to visit.

Part 2: Simulated Discussion (2-3 minutes)

You and your partner are going to talk about something together. Look at the ideas below and discuss them with your partner.

Your class wants to organise a surprise party for a teacher. Talk about these ideas and decide which ones to choose.
🎂 Make or buy a cake
🎵 Choose music to play
🎁 Buy a class present
🎈 Put up decorations
📸 Take photos at the party
Decision question: Which two things are most important to plan first?

Part 3: Extended Turn (2-3 minutes per candidate)

Each candidate receives a photograph. Describe what you can see in the photograph and answer the question about it. Your partner will then answer a short follow-up question.

Candidate A — Describe this photograph and answer the question:
📸

Photo A: A group of friends having a picnic in a park

Question: Tell me what you can see in this photograph.
Candidate B — follow-up: Do you enjoy having picnics?
Candidate B — Describe this photograph and answer the question:
📸

Photo B: A family cooking a meal together in a kitchen

Question: Tell me what you can see in this photograph.
Candidate A — follow-up: Do you like cooking? Why or why not?

Part 4: General Conversation (3 minutes)

The examiner asks questions that develop the theme of food and socialising. Give your opinions with reasons and examples.

1. Do you think it's important to eat meals together with family or friends? Why?
2. What is a popular dish in your country? Can you describe it?
3. Do you prefer eating at home or in a restaurant? Why?
4. Some people say cooking is a good hobby. What do you think?
5. How do you think eating habits might change in the future?
