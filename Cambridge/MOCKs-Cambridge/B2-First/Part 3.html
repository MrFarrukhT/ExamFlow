Stimulus for Questions 11–15

Zoe Stephens

I've been an actor for 30 years, working in TV, film and on stage. I've worked hard to get where I am, but I see young actors today who want success without proper training. Some become huge screen stars within two years, earning a lot of money and attention, but can they hold an audience? Natural talent without knowledge and technique isn't enough.


On my last job, the research and training was no more than a chat with the director over a meal the night before filming began. When I started, we did two scenes a day. Now we do six or seven. Modern screen actors are often treated like children – 'Put on these clothes, say your lines – we'll do all the rest.'


I've had to get used to people approaching me in the street, which I still find unexpected. I love it when people tell me a performance has helped them in some way – it's great to feel like I've done something useful. But I hate being photographed secretly and put in a magazine looking terrible – that's something I didn't have to worry about in the early days of my career.
