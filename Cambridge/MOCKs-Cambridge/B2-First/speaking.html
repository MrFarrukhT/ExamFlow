RECORDING IN PROGRESS

Part 1: Interview (2 minutes)

The examiner asks each candidate questions in turn. Answer fully, giving reasons and examples where possible.

1. Where are you from? What do you like about living there?
2. Do you prefer spending your free time indoors or outdoors? Why?
3. Tell me about something you learned to do recently.
4. How important is it for you to keep in touch with friends? Why?
5. What kind of things do you enjoy reading?
6. Do you think it's important to try new experiences? Why or why not?

Part 2: Long Turn (4 minutes)

Each candidate receives two photographs. Compare the photos and answer the question about them. Speak for about 1 minute. When your partner finishes, answer the follow-up question briefly (about 30 seconds).

Candidate A — Compare these photographs and answer the question:
📸

Photo A: People studying together in a university library

📸

Photo B: A young person studying alone at home with a laptop

Question: Why might these people have chosen to study in these different places?
Candidate B — follow-up: Which of these places would you prefer to study in?
Candidate B — Compare these photographs and answer the question:
📸

Photo A: People exercising together in a park

📸

Photo B: Someone exercising alone in a gym

Question: How might these people be feeling about exercising?
Candidate A — follow-up: Do you prefer exercising alone or with other people?

Part 3: Collaborative Task (4 minutes)

Discuss the question together using the prompts below. You have 2 minutes to talk about all the options, then 1 minute to decide which activity would be most effective for most people.

How can these activities help people deal with stress?
🎵 Playing a musical instrument
🐾 Spending time with animals
🌿 Going for walks in nature
🍳 Cooking a meal from scratch
💬 Talking to a close friend
Decision question: Which activity would be most effective for most people?

Part 4: Discussion (4 minutes)

The examiner asks questions that develop the themes from Part 3. Give your opinions with reasons and examples.

1. Some people say that modern life is more stressful than in the past. Do you agree?
2. Do you think schools should teach students how to manage stress? Why?
3. Is it better to deal with problems on your own or to ask for help?
4. How important is it to have a good work-life balance?
5. What role does technology play in causing or reducing stress?
